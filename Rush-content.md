## Early History

1. Rush was formed in August 1968 in the Willowdale neighbourhood of Toronto, Ontario.
2. The original lineup consisted of guitarist Alex Lifeson, bassist/vocalist Jeff Jones, and drummer John Rutsey.
3. Alex Lifeson co-founded the band and was its only continuous member.
4. Geddy Lee replaced Jeff Jones only a month after the band formed.
5. The band was originally called "The Lost Cause" and "Projection" before settling on Rush.
6. The name "Rush" was suggested by the brother of the original drummer, John Rutsey.
7. Geddy Lee was briefly kicked out of the band in 1969.
8. Geddy Lee was born Gary Lee Weinrib.
9. "Geddy" came from his mother's pronunciation of "Gary" due to her Polish-Yiddish accent.
10. Alex Lifeson was born Aleksandar Živojinović.
11. "Lifeson" is a direct English translation of his Serbian surname, meaning "son of life".
12. The band spent their early years playing bars and covering songs like "Louie Louie" and "For Your Love".
13. They refused to play Top 40 music, determined to play their own material.
14. Their first single was a cover of Buddy Holly's "Not Fade Away" (1973).
15. The early, unreleased song "Slaughterhouse" was a hard rocker about endangered wildlife.
16. "Working Man" was already being performed live as early as 1971.
17. Original drummer John Rutsey left in 1974 due to creative differences and health issues (diabetes).
18. Neil Peart replaced John Rutsey in July 1974.
19. Neil Peart's first gig with the band was on August 14, 1974, in front of 11,000 people.
20. Neil Peart quickly became the band's primary lyricist.

## Musical Style and Evolution

21. The band is famous for its intricate, progressive rock sound produced by a trio.
22. Their early sound was heavily influenced by hard rock and blues-rock.
23. The 1976 album 2112 was a breakthrough, featuring a 20-minute title track.
24. 2112 is their most frequently played song live.
25. The band transitioned to a more synthesizer-oriented sound in the 1980s.
26. They are known for incorporating complex time signatures, such as 13/4 in "Freewill".
27. Their music often covers themes of science fiction, philosophy, and history.
28. Neil Peart's lyrics were heavily influenced by Ayn Rand, particularly on the 2112 album.
29. The song "Red Sector A" was inspired by the Holocaust experiences of Geddy Lee's mother.
30. The song "YYZ" is named after the airport code for Toronto Pearson International Airport.
31. The rhythm in "YYZ" is based on the airport's Morse code signal.
32. The band has been cited as a major influence on progressive metal bands like Dream Theater.
33. Their 1981 album Moving Pictures is often considered their commercial and critical peak.
34. The song "Tom Sawyer" was ranked one of the top rock songs of all time.
35. The band often utilized "double-neck" instruments live to cover complex arrangements.
36. Geddy Lee played bass, keyboards, and foot pedals simultaneously.
37. The song "Freewill" contains some of the highest vocals Geddy Lee ever recorded.
38. The band's lyrics often focus on individualism and intellectual freedom.
39. They often recorded albums with no overdubs, playing live in the studio.
40. The band was known for its "honest" sound, rarely using backing tracks until later tours.

## The Classic Lineup (Lee, Lifeson, Peart)

41. The trio of Lee, Lifeson, and Peart remained together from 1974 to 2018.
42. Neil Peart was famously nicknamed "The Professor".
43. Neil Peart was an avid reader and often incorporated literary references into his lyrics.
44. Alex Lifeson was the only member who stayed in the band for its entire 50-year existence.
45. The band members were often described as "best friends" who rarely fought.
46. The trio was the first rock band to be made Officers of the Order of Canada.
47. They received the Order of Canada on May 9, 1996.
48. Neil Peart's daughter, Selena, was killed in a car accident in 1997.
49. Neil Peart's wife, Jacqueline, died of cancer just one year after their daughter's death.
50. Neil Peart took a five-year hiatus from the band following these tragedies.
51. Neil Peart remarried in 2000.
52. The band returned to recording after Peart's hiatus with the album Vapor Trails (2002).
53. The band's "final" studio album was Clockwork Angels (2012).
54. The band officially retired in 2018 following Neil Peart's diagnosis with cancer.
55. Neil Peart passed away on January 7, 2020, at age 67.
56. Geddy Lee and Alex Lifeson have stated that without Peart, the band is over.
57. Neil Peart was a self-taught drummer who became considered one of the best in rock history.
58. Neil Peart's drum solos were a highlight of every live Rush show.
59. Geddy Lee is highly respected for his technical ability, playing bass with his fingers.
60. Alex Lifeson was ranked 98th on Rolling Stone's list of 100 greatest guitarists.

## Live Shows and Legacy

61. The band was renowned for their marathon, 3-hour live performances.
62. Early in their career, they opened for bands like KISS, who mentored them.
63. They were one of the first bands to use extensive lighting and video technology on tour.
64. Their equipment van was once stolen on their first trip to Florida.
65. In the 1970s, they were named "Official Ambassadors Of Music" by the Canadian government.
66. The band has been nominated for seven Grammy Awards.
67. They have won ten Juno Awards (Canadian equivalent of Grammys).
68. Rush was inducted into the Canadian Music Hall of Fame in 1994.
69. They were finally inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2013.
70. As of 2022, they have sold over 42 million albums worldwide.
71. They have 24 gold records and 14 platinum (3 multi-platinum) records in the U.S.
72. They rank 5th for most consecutive gold or platinum studio albums by a rock band.
73. They have 12 Top 10 albums on the Billboard 200.
74. The band's fan base is known for being extremely loyal and dedicated.
75. The band was featured on a 2010 documentary titled Rush: Beyond the Lighted Stage.
76. Geddy Lee published a book on his love of bass guitars, titled "Geddy Lee's Big Beautiful Book of Bass".
77. The band's lyrics often explored the idea of fate vs. free will.
78. Rush was known for its "intellectual" approach to rock music.
79. They were often considered a "cult" band, despite their massive commercial success.
80. Their live sound was frequently praised for being as good as, or better than, their studio recordings.

## Trivia and Personal Facts

81. Alex Lifeson has a pilot's license.
82. Neil Peart was a passionate motorcyclist, riding between tour stops.
83. Geddy Lee is a massive fan of baseball and collects autographed memorabilia.
84. Geddy Lee was once asked to play bass for the band Yes.
85. The band's early manager, Ray Danniels, was a crucial part of their career for decades.
86. Geddy Lee and Alex Lifeson have occasionally played together since 2020, including for the Taylor Hawkins Tribute Concerts.
87. The band has been known to use humorous video skits during their live shows.
88. Neil Peart often used a specialized drum kit with a 360-degree setup.
89. The band has had over 160 original songs recorded.
90. Early in their career, they opened for Nazareth in the UK.
91. Geddy Lee often wore a "kimono" style shirt on stage in the late 70s.
92. The band has never had a #1 hit on the Billboard Hot 100, but their albums often charted in the top 10.
93. The song "Limelight" is about Neil Peart's struggle with fame.
94. Alex Lifeson released a solo album titled Victor in 1996.
95. Geddy Lee released a solo album titled My Favourite Headache in 2000.
96. The band's Snakes & Arrows album was released in 2007.
97. They are known for a strong, loyal fanbase in both North America and Europe.
98. The band's music has been featured in numerous TV shows and movies, such as Trailer Park Boys.
99. Rush's influence extends to many modern rock bands, including Foo Fighters.
100. Despite all the changes in music, they stayed true to their "power trio" format for 40+ years.
